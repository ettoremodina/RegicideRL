Related Work

Prior works in model-based RL fall into several categories:

  • Observation models (e.g., Dyna-style models) explicitly learn environment dynamics by predicting future observations and rewards, but struggle with high-dimensional inputs and accumulating errors.
  • Latent models (e.g., standard MuZero) predict values, policies, and rewards in a latent space, which is highly effective but assumes deterministic dynamics.
  • Stochastic latent models (e.g., VQ-VAE for planning) attempt to handle stochasticity, but previously failed to match MuZero's performance in combined planning and learning, or required supervised expert data.

Contributions

Algorithmic Innovations
  • Factored Dynamics: Separates transitions into deterministic afterstates and stochastic chance outcomes.
  • VQ-VAE Integration: Learns discrete chance codes end-to-end within the RL loop.
Impact
  • Extends MuZero to stochastic and partially observed domains.
  • Removes the need for perfect simulators in complex stochastic games like Backgammon and 2048.
